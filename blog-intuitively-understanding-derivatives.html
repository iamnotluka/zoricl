Intuitively understanding derivatives

2026-04-28

Let's assume:

$$f(x) = 3x^2 - 4x + 5$$

$f(3) = 20$.

If we plot this function, we'll get some sort of a parabola.

So what is a derivative and what is it telling us about the function?

The general definition is:

$$L = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(X+h) - f(X)}{h}$$

What this basically tells us is how the change in $X$ affects the function output, more accurately: the derivative measures how sensitive the output is to a tiny change in the input.

So we can pick a number $X$ and a function $f$ where $X$ is a term in a function $f$ to see what the relationship is between the change in $X$ and output of $f(X)$.

Let's say $X = 2$.

$$f(2) = 3 \cdot 2^2 - 4 \cdot 2 + 5 = 9$$

Let's take a small step forwards, $h = 0.001$.

What would be the difference? Larger or smaller do we expect? If $X$ has gone up by $0.001$, what would be the change?

So let's test it out:

$$f(2+h) = f(2.001) = 3 \cdot 2.001^2 - 4 \cdot 2.001 + 5 = 9.008003$$

So our difference is:

$$f(2+h) - f(2) = 0.008003$$

To get the ratio, which is answering if my value went up by $0.008003$ by bumping my term by $h$:

$$\frac{0.008003}{h} = \frac{0.008003}{0.001} = 8.003$$

Meaning, around $X = 2$, for a very small increase in $X$, the output increases by roughly 8 times that increase. So if $h = 0.001$ expected change is roughly $0.008$.

Let's now see what happens as $h$ approaches $0$, instead of choosing a specific small value like $0.001$.

We can sub in the whole function into that.

Let's look at our function again:

$$f(x) = 3x^2 - 4x + 5$$

And the derivative definition is:

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$$

Let's first find $f(x+h)$ when $h$ approaches and becomes $0$.

$$\begin{aligned} f(x+h) &= 3(x+h)^2 - 4(x+h) + 5 \\ &= 3(x^2 + 2xh + h^2) - 4x - 4h + 5 \\ &= 3x^2 + 6xh + 3h^2 - 4x - 4h + 5 \end{aligned}$$

From the function, we can minus the terms $3x^2$, $4x$ and $5$.

And we are left with:

$$6xh - 4h$$

Given we are dividing by $h$, we can simply get those out of the equation and get $6x - 4$.

And $6x - 4$ at $2$ is really $12 - 4$ which is $8$… very close to our $8.003$!

So at point $2$, for a very small change of $h$ the output changes $8.003$ times that $h$, and in our case, for $h = 0.001$, the value of $f(x)$ changed $h$ times that $8.003$, which was $0.008003$ and our output of $f(2.001)$ was $9.008003$!
