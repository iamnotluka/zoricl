Everything is just an interpretation

2025-12-19

This is my interpretation of Book 1, Chapter 1 of Lao Tzu's Tao Te Ching.

The Tâo that can be trodden is not the enduring and unchanging Tâo. The name that can be named is not the enduring and unchanging name.

(Conceived of as) having no name, it is the Originator of heaven and earth; (conceived of as) having a name, it is the Mother of all things.

Always without desire we must be found,
If its deep mystery we would sound;
But if desire always within us be,
Its outer fringe is all that we shall see.

Under these two aspects, it is really the same; but as development takes place, it receives the different names. Together we call them the Mystery. Where the Mystery is the deepest is the gate of all that is subtle and wonderful.

— Lao Tzu, Tao Te Ching, ch. 1 (trans. James Legge, 1891)

If you can fully express "The Way (Tao)" in words, it's already not a real thing, only a description/interpretation.

Language is a map. The Tao is the terrain.

Tao is the truth/order/process of reality.

Any formulation, doctrine or system is at best a pointer, never the thing itself. So don't confuse concepts about reality with reality.

Naming is carving reality into categories by naming them: "this is X, this is Y…"

The moment you name something, you limit it to a role, function or a label.

Reality precedes labels. Whatever "true" identity things have is prior to and deeper than any name you give them. Names are useful for coordination, but misleading when you treat them as the ultimate truth.

All is just an interpretation, an interface, a map, where truth is the foundation/the Tao which cannot be described.

Before any distinctions or naming, there is a unified undivided "foundation"/"suchness".

From that undivided ground, all structured reality emerges. The source is beyond words, beyond categories, labels or "things".

Once you start distinguishing and naming, you get multiplicity: this person, that object, this event, that identity.

The act of naming generates the world of separate entities.

So the nameless is the origin, fundamental, naming gives birth to the manifested, differentiated world.

With desires, you do not see what is.

Because clinging and grasping for things that are labeled makes your perception biased.

You don't see "what it is", you see what you "want or fear".

In that non grasping state, you perceive the deeper layer: the underlying pattern, the "secrets" of the Tao, in other words the truth/foundation/process of reality.

You should be desireless, empty, quiet and receptive — however, allow yourself to have desires.

Because without any desire at all, you might see the depth, but ignore the surface play.

With desire, you participate and see how Tao manifests as change, movement and drama.

This is the active, worldly mode: acting, doing, creating, failing, learning.

So you need both:

  • Desireless: to see the underlying structure
  • Desireful: to see how it appears as that which we label as reality

Those two modes are exactly the same because they are just different views of one reality.

We call them different things, and they feel different from the inside.

Contemplation and action, emptiness and form, depth and surface are not two separate interpretations.

They are exactly the same process looked at from different angles.

The division is simply conceptual.

Because both the deep source and its expression are ultimately the same Tao. You can't fully explain both either.

The more you look, the more layered it is: any explanation you give raises further questions.

Reality is not fully exhaustible by concepts — what we interpret is simply an interpretation and nothing else. The more you look at an interpretation, it simply renders itself as an interpretation of an interpretation — you never will get to the fundamental.

Because fundamental is the Tao, and Tao is nameless, that which cannot be described.

Holding both perspectives together — non-grasping insight into the nameless source yet engaged participant in the named world — is how you should operate.

If you only live in the desire and naming, you are trapped on the surface.

If you only live in detachment and nameless, you're disconnected from life.

The "gateway" is the stance that sees they are one and moves fluidly between them.

This stance is what opens the "manifold secrets".
